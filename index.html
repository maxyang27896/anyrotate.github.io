Multi-axis in-hand object rotation invariant to gravity direction using touch.

Abstract

In-hand manipulation represents an integral component of human dexterity. Humans rely on tactile feedback to provide stable and reactive motions to ensure objects do not slip away unintentionally during manipulation. For a robot hand to achieve the same level of dexterity requires extracting and utilizing rich contact information for precise motor control. In this paper, we present AnyRotate, a system for gravity-invariant multi-axis in-hand object rotation using touch. We leverage explicit contact features in simulation to provide tactile feedback for training a policy using Reinforcement Learning. We perform zero-shot policy transfer to reality by training an observation model to bridge the sim-to-real gap. In our real world experiment, we successfully demonstrated that our policy trained with touch can generalize to multiple hand directions for rotating various objects in arbitrary rotation axes. Interestingly, we found that despite not having explicit slip detection, multi-fingered tactile sensing can implicitly detect object movement and provide reactive behaviour that improves on the robustness of the policy, highlighting the importance of information-rich tactile sensing for dynamic manipulation.
